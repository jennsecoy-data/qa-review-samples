# Process Improvement Notes

This sample shows how repeated quality issues can be documented and reviewed to identify workflow improvements, reduce errors, and support more consistent final outputs.

## Purpose

Process improvement notes help track repeated issues that appear during QA review. Instead of only correcting one issue at a time, this type of documentation helps identify patterns that may need a checklist update, clearer instruction, additional training, or workflow change.

This type of note is useful for:

- Tracking repeated QA issues
- Identifying workflow gaps
- Improving documentation accuracy
- Reducing rework
- Supporting clearer communication
- Improving review consistency
- Helping teams meet deadlines with fewer repeated corrections

## Issue Pattern Review

### PI-001: Missing Accessibility Notes

The same type of issue appears across multiple online versions. The paper version includes the required accessibility note, but the online version does not.

**Impact:**  
This creates inconsistency between formats and may affect usability or compliance.

**Suggested improvement:**  
Add a required accessibility-note checkpoint to the final QA checklist before approval.

---

### PI-002: Caption Differences Across Forms

Image captions are mostly correct, but punctuation or wording differs between related forms.

**Impact:**  
This can reduce consistency and create confusion when materials are expected to match.

**Suggested improvement:**  
Use a single approved caption source and verify all versions against that source during review.

---

### PI-003: Outdated Wording in Ancillary Materials

Main documents are updated, but supporting materials sometimes keep older wording.

**Impact:**  
This creates mismatched information across related materials.

**Suggested improvement:**  
When an edit request is received, confirm whether related ancillary materials also need review.

---

### PI-004: Formatting Drift After Edits

Some formatting issues appear after content edits are applied.

**Impact:**  
Spacing, headings, tables, or lists may become inconsistent after updates.

**Suggested improvement:**  
Add a final formatting pass after all text and content edits are complete.

---

### PI-005: Unclear Issue Notes

Some issue notes do not clearly explain what changed, where the issue appears, or what action is needed.

**Impact:**  
Unclear notes can slow down correction and follow-up.

**Suggested improvement:**  
Use a standard issue note format:

- Issue found
- Location
- Source or requirement conflict
- Recommended correction
- Status

## Recommended Checklist Additions

Based on the sample issues above, the QA checklist could be improved by adding:

- Confirm accessibility notes are included in all required versions.
- Compare captions against one approved source.
- Check ancillary materials when main content changes.
- Complete a final formatting pass after all edits are applied.
- Use a standard format for documenting QA issues.
- Track repeated issues separately from one-time corrections.

## Example Improvement Summary

Several repeated QA issues were identified during review. The most common issues involved missing accessibility notes, inconsistent captions, outdated wording in supporting materials, and formatting differences after edits were applied.

Recommended improvements include adding specific checklist checkpoints, using one approved source for repeated content, reviewing related materials during edit verification, and standardizing QA issue notes. These changes may reduce repeated corrections and improve consistency across final materials.
