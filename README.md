## Sample QA Review Checklist

### Accuracy

- Does the final content match the approved source?
- Are names, dates, numbers, labels, item references, and instructions correct?
- Are all requested edits applied accurately?
- Are there any mismatches between versions or formats?
- Is the information supported by the provided source material?

### Completeness

- Are all required sections included?
- Are any fields, images, captions, labels, or instructions missing?
- Does the final version meet the full task requirement?
- Are paper, online, alternate-format, and supporting materials aligned when applicable?

### Consistency

- Is formatting consistent across sections?
- Are terms, titles, capitalization, punctuation, and spacing used consistently?
- Do repeated items follow the same structure?
- Are visual elements, tables, lists, and instructions presented in a consistent way?

### Content Quality

- Is the language clear, professional, and easy to understand?
- Are there spelling, grammar, readability, or formatting issues?
- Are images, artwork, captions, and layout elements placed correctly?
- Is the content appropriate for the intended audience?

### Issue Documentation

When an issue is found, I document:

- What the issue is
- Where the issue appears
- What source or requirement it conflicts with
- Whether it is a one-time issue or repeated pattern
- Recommended next action
- Final status after review

## Example QA Issue Categories

| Category | Example Issue | Possible Impact |
|---|---|---|
| Accuracy | Incorrect date, number, label, or instruction | Could cause user confusion or incorrect processing |
| Completeness | Missing section, field, caption, or required note | Final output may not meet requirements |
| Consistency | Formatting or wording differs across versions | Reduces quality and professionalism |
| Accessibility | Alternate-format note missing or inconsistent | May affect usability or compliance |
| Documentation | Issue not clearly tracked or explained | Makes follow-up and correction harder |
| Process | Same issue appears repeatedly | Indicates a workflow improvement opportunity |

## Example Review Summary

A completed QA review should clearly explain what was checked, what was found, and what action is recommended.

Example:

> Reviewed the final version against the approved source content. Most requested edits were applied correctly. One inconsistency was found in the image caption on Sample Form B, where punctuation differs from the approved wording. One online item is missing the required accessibility note. Recommend correcting both items before final approval.
